## Design Context

### Users
- Security leaders, SOC managers, and engineering teams evaluating a Detection Engineer for consulting, full-time roles, or collaboration.
- Peer practitioners from Hack The Box and security communities looking for practical write-ups and automation insights.
- Recruiters and technical stakeholders scanning quickly on mobile first, then deep-diving on desktop.

### Brand Personality
- Precise, credible, and operator-level.
- Calm confidence over hype.
- Tactical and modern with measurable outcomes.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Tactical editorial cyber aesthetic: clean dark neutrals with high-legibility accents, terminal-inspired details, and restrained motion.
- Desktop-first density with strong mobile adaptation.
- Anti-references: generic neon cyberpunk clutter, repetitive card grids, and template-like KPI hero blocks.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with evidence: measurable impact and real projects before decoration.
2. Keep cognitive load low: concise copy, clear labels, and predictable interaction patterns.
3. Build visual rhythm: intentional spacing contrast, asymmetry, and sectional pacing.
4. Motion with purpose: feedback, orientation, and emphasis only; always honor reduced motion.
5. Production readiness: accessible semantics, resilient states, and domain-ready metadata.
